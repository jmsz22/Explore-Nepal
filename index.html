About Nepal

Discover the rich heritage and natural wonders of this Himalayan nation

A Land of Extraordinary Diversity

Nepal, officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, is a landlocked country in South Asia, located mainly in the Himalayas. It is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha, making it a significant pilgrimage destination for Buddhists worldwide.

The country is home to eight of the world's ten highest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth. From the subtropical jungles of the Terai to the icy peaks of the Himalayas, Nepal offers an incredible range of landscapes, cultures, and experiences.

With over 125 ethnic groups speaking more than 120 languages, Nepal represents one of the world's most diverse cultural tapestries. Ancient temples, living goddesses, and festivals celebrating everything from seasons to deities make this country a living museum of Asian heritage.
